2Google & SEO setup Google

This is the most important section for getting found. There are three parts — all done from your Google account.

2A · Google Business Profile (your Maps listing)

This is the single biggest driver of "eyebrow near me" results. Make us a manager so we can optimise it with you:

  1. Go to business.google.com and sign in with the account that owns the listing.
  2. Open your business → Settings (gear icon) → People & access (or "Managers").
  3. Click Add → enter our email (top of page) → choose role Manager → Invite.
  4. Make sure the listing is verified (look for the verified badge). If it isn't, Google will mail/SMS you a code — complete that step.
If you've never claimed it Search your business name on Google Maps → click "Claim this business" / "Own this business?" and follow the verification. Then do the steps above.

2B · Google Search Console (proves you own the website)

This lets Google index your new site quickly. Two easy options — pick one:

  • Option 1 (simplest): after we verify the site, we give you a short verification code; you do nothing else. — handled by us once the domain is live.
  • Option 2 (you add us): if you set up Search Console yourself, open it → Settings → Users and permissions → Add user → enter our email → permission Owner or Full.

2C · Google API key for live reviews also powers Job 3

This one key switches on the automatic, real-time Google reviews on your site. It takes about 10 minutes and needs a card on file (usage is effectively free at your volume, but Google requires one).

  1. Go to console.cloud.google.com → create a project (call it "Emcey Brows").
  2. Open APIs & Services → Library → search "Places API (New)" → Enable.
  3. Open Billing → add a payment card to the project (required by Google).
  4. Open APIs & Services → Credentials → Create credentials → API key.
  5. Copy the key (starts with AIza…) and send it to us privately. We lock it down and install it safely server-side — it never appears in your website's public code.
Don't want to touch billing? No problem — your reviews still show on the site via the "Read / Write a review" buttons that link to your Google page. The API key only adds the automatic live-review display. You can switch it on anytime later.

3Real-time testimonials & reviews

3A · Google reviews Google

  • Provide the API key from step 2C (that's all that's needed — reviews then appear and refresh automatically).
  • Send your Google Maps listing link: on Maps, open your business → Share → Copy link.
  • Keep asking happy clients for a Google review and reply to each — this is what actually grows your rating over time.
Good to know Google shows up to 5 reviews at a time and chooses which ones — that's a Google rule, not a limit of your site. For specific favourites, send them to us and we'll feature them as hand-picked testimonials too.

3B · Facebook reviews Facebook

Facebook no longer lets any website pull its reviews automatically (Meta locked this down). So here's the realistic, durable approach:

  1. Confirm you have a Facebook Page (a business page — not your personal profile).
  2. Turn Reviews/Recommendations ON: on your Page → Settings → Privacy → Page and tagging (or Templates and tabs → enable the Reviews tab).
  3. Send us your Page link and your Reviews tab link (usually facebook.com/YourPageName/reviews). We point the site's Facebook buttons straight at them.
  4. When a client leaves a great recommendation, screenshot it or copy the text and send it over — we'll feature your best ones as testimonials on the site.
Optional: if you'd like us to help manage the Page, add our email as an admin/editor under Page → Settings → Page roles / New Pages experience → People with access.
